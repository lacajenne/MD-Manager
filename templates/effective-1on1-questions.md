# Effective 1-on-1 Questions

**Date**: 2025-12-15  
**Tags**: #leadership #1on1 #communication

## Summary
Collection of effective questions to use during 1-on-1 meetings to build trust, uncover issues early, and support team member growth.

## Details

### Main Points
- Open-ended questions encourage deeper conversations
- Different types of questions serve different purposes
- Adapt questions to the individual and context
- Listen more than you talk

### Key Insights
- The best 1-on-1s happen when the team member does most of the talking
- Asking about energy/motivation often surfaces issues before they become critical
- Career development questions shouldn't only happen during performance reviews

## Question Categories

### Check-in & Well-being
- "How are you feeling about work this week?"
- "What's been energizing you lately? What's been draining?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your current workload?"

### Project & Work
- "What's been your biggest challenge this week?"
- "Is there anything blocking your progress?"
- "What's something you're proud of recently?"

### Career Development
- "What skills do you want to develop?"
- "What kind of work would you like to be doing more/less of?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 6-12 months?"

### Team & Process
- "How's your relationship with [team member]?"
- "What's one thing we could improve as a team?"
- "Is there any feedback you haven't shared?"

### Manager Feedback
- "What's one thing I could do better to support you?"
- "Am I giving you enough context about team/company priorities?"
