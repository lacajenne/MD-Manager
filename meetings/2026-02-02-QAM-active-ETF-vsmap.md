# Meeting Notes

## 1. Value Stream Mapping Exercise for Active ETF Delivery
Artur and Dawid led a comprehensive review of the value stream mapping exercise conducted for the Active ETF delivery to Quantum. They detailed the mapping process, team involvement, and the resulting process flow, with contributions from Paul, Richard, Aleksandra, Giuseppe, and Natalia. *(01:48)*

## 2. Identified Challenges and Pain Points in the Delivery Process
Dawid presented a detailed analysis of the main challenges encountered during the Active ETF delivery, including:
- Unclear ownership
- Aggressive timelines
- Tool fragmentation
- Late involvement of key teams
- Difficulties in testing and handover

Further discussion and validation came from Artur, Paul, and Richard. *(13:51)*

## 3. Recommendations and Next Steps for Process Improvement
Paul, Aleksandra, Artur, and Dawid discussed the need to:
- Prioritise identified problems
- Focus on root cause analysis
- Genericise the process for broader applicability
- Streamline team involvement

Plans include reviewing similar processes in other teams and incorporating dependency mapping. *(25:26)*

## 4. Discussion on Roles, Project Focus, and Standardisation
Richard and Paul raised questions about:
- The need for standard roles
- Business analyses
- The appropriateness of project management approaches
- The shift from project-based to standardised build, deploy, and run models

Dawid and Artur provided responses, clarifying current gaps and future direction. *(21:41)*

## 5. Clarification of Value Stream Mapping Purpose and Approach
Giuseppe sought clarification on the purpose and methodology of value stream mapping. Dawid explained that the exercise aimed to analyse historical delivery, identify pain points, and develop repeatable, generic processes for future outcomes. *(26:37)*
